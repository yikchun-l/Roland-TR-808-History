The Roland TR-808: House Always Wins

Which genres of music heavily utilised the Roland TR-808? From one user-compiled Discogs list, we can see what a snippet of its wide influence looks like.

By Yikchun Lam

Published July 14, 2022

Do you recognise any of these sounds?

No? You might recognise them if those sounds were arranged like this, though.

The year is 1982, and Afrika Bambaataa has just released "Planet Rock". "Planet Rock" is just one of the many records that the Roland TR-808 has lent its sounds to. But it wasn't always this popular. In fact, it was a complete failure. Released sometime between 1980 and 1983 by the Roland Corporation, the TR-808 Rhythm Composer featured 16 programmable keys, with options to modify the sound more by changing the attack, decay, and volume of each key.

These sounds, layered together, would create a track. In those early days of electronic music, it was rare for a drum machine to be able to replicate an analog sound (someone playing an actual set of drums, for example). The 808 didn't manage to do that either, and the general weirdness of it, combined with the fact that the semiconductors that the 808 used eventually went out of production, meant that it was a commercial flop.

Until Planet Rock came along.

Crate Digging

There were a few playlists on Spotify and other platforms that supposedly featured tracks that used the 808's beats, but these were all top hits. Where were the rest of the tracks? Fortunately, I stumbled upon a list by Discogs user disman00911 — a list that was, at the time of my analysis, 1482 items long. (The list has since been updated again). Using the Discogs API, I pulled the list of records and went through the items to see what I could find. Here we go!

Figuring out where to start in this giant list of songs was a challenge, but let's start with the obvious first -- when were these records released?

As of late, there's been a resurgence of house in mainstream music - think Drake's “Sticky” and “Massive” off his latest album, Honestly, Nevermind, and Beyoncé's hit recession anthem Break My Soul - so we might see the second coming of the 808.

I think that there's a lot more that can be done with this dataset. Perhaps the next step would be to see how many charted songs make use of the 808, or which artist has the most songs that feature the 808.

Methodology

List of records retrieved using the Discogs API, data analysis performed using Python and Pandas in Jupyter Notebook. Additional research via SMD and the official Roland website.
